About Us

Wattpad is an online storytelling platform that allows users to upload and read stories written by amateur writers. It was founded in 2006 by Allen Lau and Ivan Yuen, two software engineers from Toronto, Canada.The initial idea behind Wattpad was to create a platform where people could easily share and discover stories on their mobile devices. In its early years, Wattpad primarily focused on fan fiction and works of young adult fiction. The platform gained popularity among young readers and aspiring writers,offering a space where they could showcase their creative talents and engage with a supportive community.Wattpad's user-friendly interface and mobile app contributed to its rapid growth.Over time, Wattpad expanded its offerings, allowing users to publish stories in various genres, including romance,science fiction, fantasy, mystery, and more. It also introduced multimedia features, such as the ability to include images, videos, and soundtracks in stories. In 2009, Wattpad launched its monetization program called Wattpad Stars, which aimed to support and reward popular writers on the platform. This initiative helped establish Wattpad as a platform that not only fostered creativity but also provided opportunities for writers to gain recognition and even pursue professional writing careers. As Wattpad continued to grow, it attracted the attention of traditional publishing and entertainment industries. In 2014, Wattpad announced a partnership with publishing company Sourcebooks to turn some of its popular stories into physical books. This marked the beginning of Wattpad's exploration of traditional publishing avenues. In 2017, Wattpad launched Wattpad Studios, a division focused on adapting Wattpad stories into movies, TV shows, and digital media. This move further solidified Wattpad's position as a platform that could launch the careers of aspiring writers and connect them with entertainment industry opportunities. Notably, the popular book series "After" by Anna Todd originated as a Wattpad story before being published and adapted into a successful film franchise. Wattpad's growth and influence continued, and by 2020, the platform had amassed over 90 million users worldwide. In January 2021, Wattpad was acquired by Naver, a South Korean internet conglomerate known for its search engine and webtoon platform. The acquisition provided Wattpad with additional resources and opportunities to expand its reach globally. Today, Wattpad remains a prominent platform for writers and readers alike, fostering a vibrant community of storytellers and providing a platform for diverse voices to be heard. It continues to evolve, embracing new technologies and partnerships to stay at the forefront of digital storytelling.
